Paneer Butter Masala

Description

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as butter paneer is a rich & creamy curry made with paneer, spices, onions, tomatoes, cashews and butter. As the name denotes, the curry is cooked in butter which imparts it's characteristic buttery flavor to the dish. It is one of the most popular dishes in Indian restaurants similar to kadai paneer, Palak Paneer, Matar Paneer and Malai Kofta.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 cup onions
  • 1 1/2 to 2 cups tomatoes finely chopped
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 12 to 14 cashew nuts
  • 3/4 to 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder
  • 3/4 to 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 small bay leaf
  • 1/2 inch cinnamon piece
  • 2 green cardamom
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 1/2 to 2 tablespoon butter
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 200 to 250 grams paneer
  • 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves
  • 3 tablespoon cream
  • 2 tablespoon corianer leaves

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add green cardamoms & onions. Stir fry until they turn transparent to light pink. (If you do not have ginger garlic paste for this recipe, you may add chopped ginger and garlic along with onions)
  2. Next, add tomatoes & salt. Saute for 3 minutes. Cover and cook till the onion & tomatoes turn soft & completely mushy.
  3. Add garam masala, coriander powder, cashew nuts, red chili powder & sugar (optional).
  4. Stir fry this till the onion tomato masala turns aromatic. This takes 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Turn off the stove & cool this completely.
  6. Transfer to a blender & pour water. Blend to a really silky smooth puree. If it is not smooth, filter it through a strainer to another bowl. Set this aside.
  7. Heat butter in the same pan. Optional - When the butter melts, add cinnamon, cloves, green cardamoms & bay leaf.
  8. Reduce the flame to low, and saute ginger garlic paste for a minute until it smells aromatic.
  9. Stir in the blended onion tomato puree. Taste test your puree & optionally you may add more red chili powder for a deeper color.
  10. Pour more water & mix well. Bring it to a rolling boil. Reduce the heat to medium and cook covered until the gravy cooks down to a thicker consistency. Keep stirring every few minutes.
  11. You know the gravy is done when it turns thick and traces of fat are visible on the gravy. Taste test and add more salt or garam masala if needed.
  12. Lower the flame completely, and add cubed paneer. Take dried fenugreek leaves to your palm and crush it in between your palms. Sprinkle this.
  13. Mix & cook covered on low heat for 2 minutes. Turn off and pour cream (save some for garnish) & stir.
  14. Transfer paneer butter masala to a serving bowl. Garnish with more cream & chopped coriander leaves. Serve this with butter naan, Basmati rice, or jeera rice.
